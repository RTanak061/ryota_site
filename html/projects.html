Ray Tracer

Incorporating lighting (including reflection and refraction) and shadows to 3D models and rendering them using the stb_image library in C++.

Used the Phong reflection model for lighting: $$I_p = k_a i_a + \sum_{m} \left( k_d (\hat{L}_m \cdot \hat{N}) i_{m,d} + k_s (\hat{R}_m \cdot \hat{V})^{\alpha} i_{m,s} \right)$$ $$ \begin{aligned} I_p &:\; \text{illumination at surface point } p \\ k_a, k_d, k_s &:\; \text{ambient, diffuse, and specular material coefficients} \\ i_a &:\; \text{ambient light intensity} \\ i_{m,d}, i_{m,s} &:\; \text{diffuse and specular intensity of light source } m \\ \hat{L}_m &:\; \text{direction vector from surface point to light } m \\ \hat{N} &:\; \text{surface normal vector} \\ \hat{R}_m &:\; \text{perfect reflection direction} \\ \hat{V} &:\; \text{view direction} \\ \alpha &:\; \text{shininess coefficient} \end{aligned} $$

Generating Random Trees Using L-System Fractals

Tree geometry was described using L-system grammar and rendered on OpenGL using mesh geometry

Depth of recursion and randomly selected angle and length of branch makes each tree unique

Leaves are generated as dragon curves (a type of fractal), making every part of a tree a fractal

Explanation of L-System: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L-system

Grammar used for this project: ("F[+F]F[-F]F", "F[+F][-F]", "FF[-F][+F]", "F[+F[+F]][-F[-F]]")
